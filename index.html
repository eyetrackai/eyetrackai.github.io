Abstract

In this paper, we present a novel, robust method for evaluating the positional accuracy of augmented virtual reality (AR/VR) based eye tracking systems. Our approach leverages a robotic head equipped with artificial eyes, coupled with a target display environment developed within the Unity game engine. This innovative setup is designed to mimic human eye movements and interactions with virtual environments, thereby providing a standardized, reproducible platform for eye tracking assessment. We detail the construction and calibration of the robotic head, as well as the integration of artificial eyes capable of replicating a wide range of eye movements. The Unity-based target display environment enables the execution of various eye tracking evaluation experiments, including the presentation of static and moving targets, among other scenarios. These experiments are carefully designed to challenge the eye tracking systems under test, covering a comprehensive spectrum of real-world use cases. The methodology for analyzing the data collected from these experiments is outlined, focusing on the assessment of positional accuracy, latency, and tracking stability across different conditions. Preliminary results demonstrate the effectiveness of our method in identifying performance discrepancies among leading AR/VR eye tracking systems, highlighting its potential as a benchmarking tool for developers and researchers alike. Furthermore, we discuss the implications of our findings for the design and improvement of eye tracking technologies in AR/VR applications. By offering a reliable and scalable evaluation framework, our method contributes significantly to the advancement of immersive technologies, paving the way for more accurate, responsive, and user-friendly AR/VR experiences.
